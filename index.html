Mapping the orbit between space data and human need

The GeoEnergy Space Nexus Research Institute builds open data infrastructure and open-source tools across three pillars: space & spatial intelligence, climate & energy & humanitarian impact, and space exploration — all documented and version-controlled on GitHub.

Three pillars

Six project areas, one open research orbit

Every repository the Institute publishes belongs to one of three pillars. Each pillar groups related project areas that share data, tooling, and documentation standards.

Why GESNRI exists

Mission & vision

Mission

Open data, real-world resilience

We advance open, collaborative research across space science, geospatial technology, climate intelligence, and sustainable energy — turning space-derived and spatial data into actionable knowledge for humanitarian needs and resilient communities worldwide.

Vision

A recognized hub for open space & geospatial research

We envision a world where space-based and spatial data are openly accessible and actively used to address climate adaptation, energy access, and humanitarian crises through transparent, community-driven research.

Every pillar, documented as a GitHub repository

All Institute repositories follow the same documentation structure — README, LICENSE, CONTRIBUTING guide, and code of conduct — in line with GitHub's community guidelines and acceptable use policies, so researchers and partners can find, use, and contribute to our work.
