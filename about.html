Organizational foundations

Mission, vision & core values

These foundations guide every repository, dataset, and tool the Institute publishes — and the partnerships we build to support climate resilience, sustainable energy, and humanitarian response.

Our mission

Open data, real-world resilience

The mission of the GeoEnergy Space Nexus Research Institute is to advance open, collaborative research at the intersection of space science, geospatial technology, climate intelligence, and sustainable energy, while ensuring that the resulting knowledge and tools directly support humanitarian needs and resilient communities worldwide.

We pursue this mission by building accessible data infrastructure, developing open-source tools, and fostering partnerships across research institutions, humanitarian organizations, and local communities — turning space-derived and spatial data into actionable knowledge for sustainable development.

Our vision

A global hub for open space & geospatial research

We envision a world where space-based and spatial data are openly accessible and actively used to address humanity's most pressing challenges — climate adaptation, energy access, and humanitarian crises — through transparent, reproducible, and community-driven research.

GESNRI aspires to be a recognized hub for open geospatial and space research, known for the quality of its data, the impact of its tools, and the breadth of its collaborative network spanning research institutions, space agencies, humanitarian bodies, and local communities.

What guides us

Core values

Seven values shape how we run our projects, share our data, and work with partners.

01

Openness

Committed to open data, open-source software, and transparent research practices, accessible to researchers, practitioners, and communities everywhere.

02

Collaboration

We value partnerships across disciplines, sectors, and borders — strengthened by contributions from institutions, agencies, and the open-source community.

03

Integrity

Rigorous scientific standards, ethical data practices, and honest reporting of methods and findings, with clear provenance and attribution.

04

Sustainability

Projects designed with long-term environmental and social impact in mind — sustainable energy, climate resilience, and responsible resource use.

05

Humanitarian focus

Research oriented toward real-world impact, so data, tools, and findings can support vulnerable communities and humanitarian response.

06

Innovation

Exploration and experimentation across space science, climate modeling, and energy planning — creative, evidence-based approaches to complex problems.

07

Accessibility

Datasets, tools, and documentation usable by researchers and practitioners at all levels, with clear documentation and inclusive design.

Implementation

Strategic goals

These goals guide implementation of our mission and vision across all project pillars and repositories.

GOAL 01

Build open data infrastructure

Establish and maintain well-documented, version-controlled repositories of space, spatial, humanitarian, and climate data, hosted and managed through GitHub in line with open data best practices.

GOAL 02

Develop open-source tools

Create and maintain open-source software for data processing, climate analytics, energy planning, and space exploration, with clear licensing and contribution pathways.

GOAL 03

Strengthen humanitarian impact

Connect research outputs to humanitarian use cases, supporting evidence-based decision-making in disaster response, resource allocation, and community resilience.

GOAL 04

Advance sustainable energy solutions

Provide geospatial tools and datasets that support the planning and expansion of renewable energy infrastructure, particularly in underserved regions.

GOAL 05

Foster open collaboration

Grow an active community of contributors, researchers, and partner organizations engaged with the Institute's repositories, following clear contribution guidelines and codes of conduct.

GOAL 06

Maintain GitHub policy compliance

Ensure that all repositories, documents, and published materials comply with GitHub's community guidelines, licensing requirements, and acceptable use policies.
