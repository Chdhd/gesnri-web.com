Project portfolio

Three pillars, six open project areas

The Institute's research is organized around three pillars. Each pillar contains project areas with shared objectives and key activities, designed to be published as GitHub repositories with README, LICENSE, and CONTRIBUTING documentation, in line with GitHub's community guidelines.

Pillar I

Space & Spatial Intelligence

Building accessible, version-controlled repositories of space-based and ground-collected geospatial data that researchers and developers can use for analysis and application development.

Project 1.1

Space Data Source

Aggregates, curates, and distributes open space-based datasets — satellite imagery, remote sensing products, orbital telemetry, and earth observation archives — into accessible, version-controlled repositories.

Objectives

  • Standardized pipelines for ingesting open satellite and earth observation data
  • GitHub-hosted catalogs and metadata indexes for discoverability
  • Reproducible scripts and notebooks for processing raw space data
  • Interoperability with GeoTIFF, NetCDF, and STAC formats

Key activities

  • Partnering with open data providers and research consortia
  • Automated data update workflows using GitHub Actions
  • Documentation and tutorials for dataset access and use
Project 1.2

Spatial Data Collection

Establishes systematic methods for collecting, validating, and organizing ground-based and aerial spatial data — field surveys, GPS tracking, drone imagery, and crowdsourced geographic information — providing ground-truth for the Space Data Source project.

Objectives

  • Open protocols for spatial data collection in field research
  • Version-controlled datasets with clear provenance and licensing
  • Integration pathways for community-contributed geographic data
  • Quality assurance frameworks for spatial dataset validation

Key activities

  • Standardized data collection templates and tools
  • GitHub repositories for dataset versioning and contribution tracking
  • Training partner organizations and volunteers on best practices

Pillar II

Climate, Energy & Humanitarian Impact

Connecting climate intelligence, sustainable energy planning, and humanitarian response through open datasets, predictive models, and community-driven analytics.

Project 2.1

Humanitarian Data Source

Develops open repositories of humanitarian data — population displacement, disaster impact assessments, food security indicators, and infrastructure vulnerability maps — supporting evidence-based humanitarian response.

Objectives

  • Unified, documented repositories of public humanitarian datasets
  • Ethical data handling, anonymization, and privacy compliance
  • Visualization tools combining humanitarian, spatial, and climate data
  • Alignment with established data standards such as HXL

Key activities

  • Open-source dashboards for humanitarian data exploration
  • Data-sharing agreements with humanitarian organizations
  • Case studies linking humanitarian needs with spatial analysis
Project 2.2

Smart-Climate Integration

Integrates climate data streams, predictive modeling, and smart sensor networks to support climate monitoring, early warning systems, and adaptive planning — bridging space data, ground sensors, and AI-driven analytics.

Objectives

  • Integrated dashboards combining satellite and IoT sensor data
  • Predictive models for climate risk assessment using open ML tools
  • Alerting systems for extreme weather and environmental hazards
  • Open standards for climate data exchange and interoperability

Key activities

  • Open-source climate analytics libraries hosted on GitHub
  • Pilot smart-sensor deployments with local communities
  • Publishing research findings and model code for community reuse
Project 2.3

Sustainable Energy Development

Supports planning, modeling, and deployment of renewable and sustainable energy systems, with a focus on geospatial site selection, resource assessment, and energy access planning for underserved regions.

Objectives

  • Geospatial models for solar, wind, and geothermal resource assessment
  • Open-source tools for energy infrastructure planning and impact analysis
  • Data-driven site recommendations for community energy access
  • Open collaboration on energy datasets and methodologies

Key activities

  • Renewable resource maps from open satellite and climate datasets
  • Open-source planning toolkits for energy access projects
  • Partnering with local stakeholders on pilot energy projects

Pillar III

Space Exploration & Innovation

Supporting research, education, and open collaboration in space exploration through simulation tools, planetary science resources, and citizen-science initiatives.

Project 3.1

Space Exploration

Develops open-source simulation tools, curates planetary science datasets, and fosters citizen-science participation in space exploration research, in collaboration with academic and space-agency partners.

Objectives

  • Open-source orbital mechanics and mission-planning simulations
  • Educational resources on planetary science and astronomy
  • Citizen-science participation in exploration research
  • Collaboration with academic and space-agency partners

Key activities

  • Open-source mission simulation and visualization tools
  • Community challenges and hackathons on exploration problems
  • Educational content and tutorials for students and researchers

How it fits together

Cross-pillar synergies & GitHub compliance

Cross-pillar synergies

Space data underpins climate and energy modeling; spatial data collection validates and refines space-derived datasets; humanitarian data benefits from both spatial and climate intelligence; and space exploration research drives innovation that feeds back into earth observation and data collection capabilities.

GitHub documentation compliance

Every project repository is maintained with clear licensing, contribution guidelines, codes of conduct, and documentation in accordance with GitHub's community guidelines and acceptable use policies — including a README, LICENSE file, and CONTRIBUTING guide.
